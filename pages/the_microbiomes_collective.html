About the project


The Microbiome is the community of microorganisms that belong to a particular environment, inside and outside of our bodies. They surround the spaces we inhabit and have the great role of keeping the balance of these ecosystems. The presence of microorganisms in these microbiomes have both positive and adverse influences on our physical health. The accelerated growth and pace of life in cities is altering the natural balance of these microbiomes, making cities into volatile habitats. How would you navigate the city every day knowing the biological impact that different spaces have on our bodies?

The Microbiome Collective is a speculative platform that makes visible the biological connection we have with spaces. Through the use of a handheld device and a mobile application, it displays how our movement through the city affects the balance of our health in relation to usually unseen microbiomes. This platform suggests a different way of seeing our urban spaces, opening possibilities of exploration and unlocking the access to our personal health.

Approach   


This phase of the MA program was structured in four pillars. These pillars were the different directions that design could take; design for enterprise, design for public systems, service design, and design for discourse. Students were asked to focus on one of these four different directions to base their thesis project on. The method used throughout the develpment of the thesis project promoted self-directed research backed up by professional mentoring and studio reviews.



Speculative Critical Design


My engagement with discoursive methods through previous projects within the MA program shaped the direction of my project from early stages. I realised that this channel could allow me to fabricate a near future scenario (Taxonomy of Futures, Dunne & Raby) were present technology could be pushed to tackle growing environmental concerns and create new ways of interfacing with our enviroment.

The Beginings


In my attempt to look for sustainable systems and alternative manufacturing, and inspired by nature, I found myself interested in areas such as sports and outdoor activities to energy, biotechnology and synergistic systems. Through the exploration of these synergistic systems I found how bacteria, that are part of our immune systems, are connected to our health.

This was the starting point of the refinement of my project. I found recent research projects that were looking into the role of bacteria and microorganisms inside our body and how we are affected by these microorganisms in our changing surroundings.

The project proposed a crowd-sourced platform for the exploration of the microbial world. It was directed to citizen scientists, in other words, curious citizens willing to learn and understand through science how everyday activities affect our bodies and behaviours or citizens that are just willing to collaborate towards the expansion of scientific knowledge through the collaboration within experiments and data gathering.

Taking the advancement in sequencing technologies and the miniaturization of laboratory equipment as the basis of the project. This platform proposed a cloud infrastructure that could process samples of different materials collected by the user to then respond through an interface that indicated the user what types of organisms they had found, where the were located and how it is related to our health.

Building Scenarios


How would a device like this be used within urban life? And how would you be able to display all this information to the user? Although very broad, these thought exercises started directing me to the construction of a visual language and framework of service. An important aspect of these stories was that through this technology I was empowering the user to read his environment in different ways.

Part of the concept at this stage aimed to design a modular system that allowed you to have different intensities of monitoring and offered flexibility in the way of use. However these directions required of a high understanding of technical aspects for the proper functioning of the device.



Technical Feasibility


Among all the research undertaken in this project I came across a series of technologies and research papers that studied and speculated about the feasibility of miniaturizing sequencing technologies. To my surprise, an English company called Oxford Nanopore Technologies had already developed the smallest DNA and RNA sequences ever created. This device offers almost real time data of DNA and RNA. This is a device that exists today with a form factor that is smaller than a smartphone.

Searcihng for a Typology


Looking into different shapes but following a simple and minimalistic language I sketched different ways that this speculative object could have always considering the technological components inside the device.

Prototyping


3D modelling allowed me quickly test the feasibility of the concept as well as the practicality. Through these shapes I wanted to communicate the reality of the device rather than speculate without having an understanding of the technical implications. Looking back at this stage I can see that this was a challenging moment. It was very difficult to define a shape to an object without falling into the realm of ‘smart objects’ that give no clue on what the context or function is.

Outcome   


The visual interface is what ties the concept together. From my perspective and for the goals of this project I emphasized the visual language inside the app to reinforce a clear communication to the user.I also focused on the whole architecture of the system. I defined the medium for interactions and built a visual language that ties together every piece of the service.

Visual Language


The majority of people’s perception of the planet is ruled by what is visible and what can be immediatly percieved. Factors as climate change and health are things that are invisible to the naked eye. This project’s first aim was to speculate about how design can help visualize this invisible forces.

The microbiome is not one microbe but a network of them, that work synergistically with its host, be it human, animal or spatial in the creation of complex interchange systems. I started to ideate how to visualize the balance of our surrounding microbiomes through 3D elements.

Chemicals


Faceted, geometric bodies are used to identify chemicals found around different environments in the city. The faces of this bodies reflect the number of substances and chemicals that have been found through scanning with the hand held device.

Organisms


Organic, liquid-like bodies are used to visualize the library of organisms found in different environments around the city. This houses benefitial and harmful microorganisms.

Indicator


The organic shape of the indicator body resembles a liquid element. Through this metaphor I wanted to communicate the vulnerability of our bodies to the environment around us. This body reacts to the user’s input and fluctuates between an altered state and a balanced state. The less diversity of bacteria the users finds the more altered the object becomes. Viruses and chemicals also affect the shape negatively. The user has the challenge to keep the object balanced by exploring their environment, which ultimately also affect their health positively.

The Home Screen


Graphic indicator that responds to the microorganisms and chemicals found. The more harmful organisms the user finds the more indicator will alter its shape.

The bacterial score is a synthesis of the balance of the organisms the users has found. An algorithm processes these findings with online data to stablish a score that represents the balance of the user’s health.

The assistant acts as an artificial intelligent bot that crosses data gathered by different users across the city, to suggest activities that can improve the user’s bacterial score.





Passive Tracking


The Threats label shows the organisms found that present a threat to the user’s helath. A geotag is created for each finding. These could take form of viruses and chemicals.

The Unknown label is a record of unkown findings. Biological data that couldn’t be classified. It could represent a new organism or error.

The Organic label represents the list of organisms found during the day.
